Scup

The scup is a fish that occurs primarily in the Atlantic from Massachusetts to South Carolina. Along with many other fish of the family Sparidae, it is also commonly known as porgy. Scup grow as large as 18 in and weigh 3–4 lb, but they average 0.5–1.0 lb

Scup has a slightly sweet, mild flavor with flaky white flesh. Their small size makes them ideal for frying or baking whole.
